Deck Breakdown

A Commander Deck is made up of 1 Commander and 99 other cards. All cards in the deck must be only one copy, except for Basic land cards; they must all also be within the color identity of the Commander. Most Commander decks will break down into groups of The Commander, Lands, Creature cards or Commander Engine-related cards, Ramp, Removal, Card draw, and "Finisher" cards. On average, there will be about 37-40 lands, 10-13 Ramp cards, 8-12 Removal cards, 7+ sources of Card draw (this will vary depending on what colors you are running), 2 "Finisher" cards, and the remaining 22-35 cards will be creature cards or other cards that synergize well with the Commander. The land cards in the deck should typically consist of more Dual color lands, the more colors you are running in your deck. Though you do not want to run entirely non-basic lands, many cards and non-basic lands require basic lands to be useful.

Tip Using Websites like Archidekt or Moxfield can be very usefull tools for deck building.

Picking your Commander

The first step to building your deck is picking your commander as this decides the color identity and strategy of your deck. Commanders must be "Legendary", most the time they are creatures, though it can also be a "Vehicle" or a "Spacecraft"(Must have a Power/Toughness box). Commanders also tend to have a Multi-color identity though there are many Mono-color Commanders. This is important because all other cards in the deck must be in the color identity of the commander and the colors you pick will limit the pool of cards you have to pick from. Many people chose their commander based on the Creature type of the card, for example Elves, Frogs, or Dragons. Others chose their commander based on the abilites it has and will build and strategy or "Engine" base on that ability that a portion of the deck is focused on strengthening. The commander is the Face of the deck and it's identity, types, and abilites are what will guide the construction of the rest of the deck.

Filling the 99

  1. Determine your deck's strategy Use your commander to determine what the goal and theme of your deck is. For example a popular Elf Commander is Lathril, Blade of the Elves, his goal is to make a bunch of Elf tokens and make them big by adding +1/+1 counters. In this deck Immaculate Magistrate is a very synergistic card as it acts as a enabler and payoff by adding +1/+1 counters to Lathril he is able to do more damage to an opponent, therefore creating more Elf tokens. Creating more tokens allows Immaculate Magistrate to make Lathril even bigger as Immaculate Magistrate adds +1/+1 counters based on the number of Elfs you control. Using abilities like this to build off and select your other cards based off will allow you to build a deck that has a cohesive stretegy instead of just a random assortment of cards.

  2. Selecting Cards There are a few things to keep in mind when selecting creature and other spell cards for the 99. Mana cost is a very iportant factor to take into account when slecting cards as depending on the colors you are playing the mana availiable can greatly vary. For examples green decks are prone to having more ramps cards to create more mana, though black decks tend to have alot of cards that can reduce the mana cost of other cards. Looking for cheap creatures that give you abilities or can get on the board quickly is going to make your deck play better for Commander since there are typically 4 players you want to be able to make the most of each turn. Also adding creature with mutiple abilities like adding mana or drawing card can be greatly beneficial to your deck.

  3. Mana Distribution Mana Distribution is very important in commander where you have 100 different cards across typically multiple colors. Making sure you have the mana to play your deck is essential in Commander as it is traditional played with a group of 4, you have to make sure you are able to make the most of each turn. The more colors in your commanders color idenity the more dual lands you will want to include in your deck, but you typiclly want to have at least a hand full of basic lands for each color in your Commanders color identity. They are also lands that are good to include that add mutliple mana or colorless mana as well. Ensuring your mana distribution is designed to allow you to play your deck will be quintissential to enjoyment of playing and competativeness of your deck.

Brackets

The concept of brackets is unique to the Commander format and is used to seperate the different competativeness levels of commander decks. There are 5 brackets within Commander, 1 being the weakest and 5 being the strongest. Brackets 1 decks, also reffered to a Exhibition decks, are typically either made of random card with no real plan, or leans into a theme or gimmick so strongly it is to the detrement of the play strengh of the deck. Though these decks can be really fun to play still as you tend to lean in to messing with the other players instead of actually trying to win.
Bracket 2 decks, also know as Core decks are the level that the average pre-constructed deck is at. Bracket 2 decks are good but do not include any "Game Changers" or infinite combos.
Bracket 3 decks, also reffered to as Upgraded, are often Pre-Constructed Decks that have been upgraded. It is pretty common for homemade decks to fall within bracket three and this is a bracket that most casual players tend to aim for when constructing a deck. Bracket 3 decks will include at least 3 Game Changer cards which are card specifically foud on the linked lsit that have been determined to be able to chnage the outcome of the game entierely on their own. Bracket 3 decks also include a 2 card late game infinite combo, meaning there is a set of two cards included that when played together will create a infinte combo and overwhelm the board state allowing the player to win.
Bracket 4 decks, also reffered to as Optimized, are decks tha may still ahve a theme but are considereded High power Commander. These decks have no restritions(other than the banned list), and will typiccally include cards that cause mass land denial or give the player extra turns.
Bracket 5 decks, also reffered to as CEDH(Competative Elder Dragon Highlander), which is the most competetive bracket in commander. The main difference bettwenn brcket 4 and 5 is the intent while playing. While Bracket 4 decks want to win in about 4-5 turns and actually play the game CEDH decks typcally do not have any theme and their goals is to win the game as quickly as possible. This is fast ruthless format that focuses on using cheap mana cost spells tp be able to win the game quickly as games in this bracket tend to be extremly quick. This bracket focuses heaily on the meta of the game and all the possible combos to be able to do as much as possible with the lowest mana cost.
