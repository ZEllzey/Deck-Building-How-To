Deck Breakdown

A Commander Deck is made up of 1 Commander and 99 other cards. All cards in the deck must be only one copy, except for Basic land cards; they must all also be within the color identity of the Commander. Most Commander decks will break down into groups of The Commander, Lands, Creature cards or Commander Engine-related cards, Ramp, Removal, Card draw, and "Finisher" cards. On average, there will be about 37-40 lands, 10-13 Ramp cards, 8-12 Removal cards, 7+ sources of Card draw (this will vary depending on what colors you are running), 2 "Finisher" cards, and the remaining 35-22 cards will be creature cards or other cards that synergize well with the Commander. The land cards in the deck should typically consist of more Dual color lands, the more colors you are running in your deck. Though you do not want to run entirely non-basic lands, many cards and non-basic lands require basic lands to be useful.

Picking your Commander

The first step to building your deck is picking your commander as this decides the color identity and strategy of your deck. Commanders must be "Legendary", most the time they are creatures, though it can also be a "Vehicle" or a "Spacecraft"(Must have a Power/Toughness box). Commanders also tend to have a Multi-color identity though there are many Mono-color Commanders. This is important because all other cards in the deck must be in the color identity of the commander and the colors you pick will limit the pool of cards you have to pick from. Many people chose their commander based on the Creature type of the card, for example Elves, Frogs, or Dragons. Others chose their commander based on the abilites it has and will build and strategy or "Engine" base on that ability that a portion of the deck is focused on strengthening. The commander is the Face of the deck and it's identity, types, and abilites are what will guide the construction of the rest of the deck.

Filling the 99

  1. Determine your deck's strategy Use your commander to determine what the goal and theme of your deck is. For example a popular Elf Commander is Lathril, Blade of the Elves, his goal is to make a bunch of Elf tokens and make them big by adding +1/+1 counters. In this deck Immaculate Magistrate is a very synergistic card as it acts as a enabler and payoff by adding +1/+1 counters to Lathril he is able to do more damage to an opponent, therefore creating more Elf tokens. Creating more tokens allows Immaculate Magistrate to make Lathril even bigger as Immaculate Magistrate adds +1/+1 counters based on the number of Elfs you control. Using abilities like this to build off and select your other cards based off will allow you to build a deck that has a cohesive stretegy instead of just a random assortment of cards.

  2. Selecting cards There are a few things to keep in mind when selecting creature and other spell cards for the 99. Mana cost is a very iportant factor to take into account when slecting cards as depending on the colors you are playing the mana availiable can greatly vary. For examples green decks are prone to having more ramps cards to create more mana, though black decks tend to have alot of cards that can reduce the mana cost of other cards. Looking for cheap creatures that give you abilities or can get on the board quickly is going to make your deck play better for Commander since there are typically 4 players you want to be able to make the most of each turn.
