Standard Deck Building

This guide will teach you the basics to how to Build a Standard format Magic Deck.

Deck Breakdown

Standard decks, on average, you want to run 23-27 lands, but the rest of the deck is heavily reliant on what kind of deck you're trying to make.

Standard Categories

Within Standard there are 4 main categiories:

Aggro

Aggressive decks that want to end the game by turn 4 or 5. They don’t run high mana cost spells because if the game gets to the point where they could be played, the deck has probably run out of steam already. Aggro decks focus on powerful early game spells that deal damage or buff their creatures so they can get a lot of damage early on and outpace their opponents

Tip The manacurve of an aggro deck should be heavily weighted towards 1-3 cost cards.

Midrange

Midrange decks try to play the game slower than aggro decks but faster than control decks. Midrange decks choose cards that provide good tempo and help them build a board without running out of resources by the midgame. They want to be able to play defensively against aggro and aggressively against control decks.

Tip The mana curve of midrange decks tends to be weighted towards 3-cost cards.

Control

The slowest playstyle common in Standard, control decks run lots of disruptive tools in order to stop their opponent’s game plan before it is allowed to come online. They want to drag the game out so they can play their high-cost cards to win the game. Most control decks are built around slowing down the most prevalent aggro decks, so most of them run cheap removal and expensive payoff cards.

Combo

Combo decks are unique in that they can fit anywhere on the speed scale set by aggro and control decks. They are heavily reliant on what combos are available in the current Standard card pool, but most forego removal and interaction cards for more card draw ways to search for their combo pieces. The point of a combo deck is to find copies of each piece of your combo and play them to win the game on the spot. My favorite combo deck in recent memory revolved around the cards Stormsplitter and Enduring Vitality. This combo sought to get both of them on the field at the same time, then play a bunch of cheap instants and sorceries that drew cards. Each time you would play one of those instants or sorceries, you would double the number of Stormsplitters you controlled, each of which could tap for mana to pay for the next instant or sorcery. Once you had enough Stormsplitters, you could attack with all of them and win the game.
