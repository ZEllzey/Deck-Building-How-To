Pre-release Deck Building

This is a guide for a to build a deck at a Magic: The Gathering Set Pre-release Event.

Deck Breakdown

The type of deck you build at a pre-relase event is considered a "limited" format deck. This format is typically played at events where there are sealed magic packs used to create a deck from, common examples of this are Drafts or Jumpstart. Limited decks are 60 cards and typically consits of about 17-19 lands, 4-5 removal cards, 4-6 instats or sorceries, and the remaining are creatures and artifacts. It is most common for pre-release decks to be two colored, though it is possible to play monocolored or three colored decks. These options are not comman though as is it rare to draw enough cards from one color to make a Monocolored deck, and three color decks run the risk on not having the necessary mana to play your cards.

How to build at a Pre-Release

What is in a pre-release kit The typical pre-release event will include 6 booster packs from the new set, a promo card, and a spin down dice.

The method to building a pre-release deck is different compared to other decks as the timelimit and card selection are very limited. At a pre-release you get your pre-release kit and an hour to build your deck. You can use any cards from your kit to build your deck, includig the promo card, and you get an hour to construct your deck. The most common method to start this is to sort your cards while opening the packs. I also tend to make a pile for any cardsI find especially interesting or good that I maybe able to build my deck off of.

I will then go through each color and take tabs of how many creatures vs other spells I have in each color. I typically decide to pick the two colors that contain the most creatures or have especially good options. From there I will start making new piles from the two colors I have chosen. I will start by picking the cards I know I want to included if they are particularly strong and then I will pick cards with cheaper mana costs or abilites that work well with each other. One of the first thing I will look for though is my removal cards as it can be common to not draw very many. From there I will begin pick the creatures by looking for cheaper creatures to build the bulk of this categroy and get more selective with the expensive mana cost creature based on their abilites. I will select other sorceries and instants from the cards as well as if there are any good colorless options. You want to be sure to never have less then 17 Lands though as it will be difficult to have enough Mana to play your deck.

One of the most important things is to pay attention to Mana cost because pick alot of high mana creature can mean you end up not being able to play anything before your opponent can take you out as you can't afford any of your creatures.

Pre-Release Events

Pre-Release events are intented to allow friendly competition while players testout the new card sets and learn any new new mechanics. They are a great way to introduce new players to the game as it is a very beginner friendly format and the competativeness is minimal. They are also a great way to start learned it buidl your own decks as it limits the card pool your can chose from instead or trying to build from unending choices which can become overwhelming to start with.
